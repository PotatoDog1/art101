Lab 4 - Pseudocoding and Problem-Solving

Challenge

Our first challenge was thinking of an every day task that students weren't too familiar with. The second challenge was surely enough to think about a computer task we wanted to pseudocode.

Problems

We had problems with trying to figure out whether our pseudocode was high level enough.

Results

We produced pseudocode for how to build a build-a-bear and computer pseudocode that details how to make a chess game, as seen below:

Task 1: Steps to build a Build-a-Bear

  1. Choose an animal that you would like to build.
  2. Decide on a sound effect to add to your animal.
  3. Pick out a heart and kiss it.
  4. Stuff the animal with stuffing and the heart that you choose.
  5. Hug your animal.
  6. Choose an outfit for your animal and dress them up.
  7. Name your animal, take the birth certificate provided.
  8. Take your animal home.

Task 2: Creating a Chess Game

  1. Print the board before each turn.
  2. Player evaluates possible moves.
  3. Accept user input.
  4. Validate user move.
  5. Evaluate possible moves for the computer.
  6. Choose a move for the computer.
  7. Test for Win! condition.
